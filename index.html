Monetary Economics

A simple visualization of China's money supply and real GDP.

Lead-Lag Correlations between Detrended Money Supply and Real GDP

Positive lags indicate that money supply leads real GDP. Negative lags indicate that money supply lags real GDP.

How to read this chart?

The chart shows correlations between detrended real GDP and detrended M0, M1, and M2 from -8 to +8 quarters. A higher correlation means stronger cyclical comovement.

Data and method

Monthly money supply data are converted to quarterly frequency and matched with quarterly real GDP. All series are detrended using the HP filter before calculating correlations.
